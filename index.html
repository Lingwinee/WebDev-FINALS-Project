IKO-Bin

A Proposed Enhanced Street Dumpster and Collection System

IKO-Bin is an intelligent waste solution for public areas. It simplifies recycling by automatically sorting and compacting waste. The bin also tracks its fill level and gathers data for efficient waste management.

A new Collection System and Technology

What is IKO-Bin?

IKO-Bin is a smart solution for Barangay Mambaling's waste management problem. With the world facing a growing waste crisis, IKO-Bin aims to improve the efficiency of waste collection. Traditional methods often lead to overflow and health risks. Our research shows that IKO-Bin can optimize waste collection, promote sustainability, and improve community health. By addressing these issues, IKO-Bin contributes to a cleaner and healthier environment for Barangay Mambaling.


  • Optimized Resource Allocation: Ensures efficient use of resources, reducing operational costs.

  • Environmental Sustainability: Promotes eco-friendly practices and reduces the environmental impact of waste.

  • Community Health: Enhances public health by preventing waste overflow and creating job opportunities in waste management.

  • Technological Advancements: Incorporates modern technology to improve waste collection efficiency, including real-time monitoring and smart scheduling.

Why is IKO-Bin important?

Improper waste disposal and the resulting accumulation have far-reaching consequences on the environment and public health. It contributes to land, water, and air pollution, which in turn accelerates climate change and disrupts ecosystems. Contaminants from waste can seep into soil and waterways, poisoning plants, animals, and humans. The toxic gases released during decomposition degrade air quality, posing respiratory risks and exacerbating global warming. Additionally, waste buildup leads to habitat destruction, threatening biodiversity and causing species loss. These issues not only harm the environment but also increase the prevalence of diseases, endanger food security, and undermine the quality of life for communities worldwide.
